# Brasa — Product Context

## Product Purpose

Brasa is a World Cup 2026 fantasy pool (bolão) for Brazilian football fans and their friends. Users predict match scores, compete on a leaderboard, and earn points through accuracy. The system auto-updates scores and calculates points via a cron job — users just play.

## Register

product

## Users

Brazilian football fans, 20–45 years old, playing with their friend group. They check the site on mobile between matches, in bars, at home watching the game, or at work sneaking a peek. High energy, competitive, emotional. They care about bragging rights more than prize money.

## Brand

**Name:** Brasa
**Tagline:** O bolão mais quente da Copa

**Personality:** Confident. Heated. Brazilian. Not corporate-sports. Think WhatsApp group chaos during a match, not FIFA official broadcast. Bold typography, dark and fiery palette, direct copy that doesn't waste words.

**Tone:** Direct, competitive, a bit provocative. "Você errou feio" not "Unfortunately your prediction was incorrect." Celebrate good predictions loudly. Don't soften losses.

## Anti-references

- Generic sports apps (ESPN, Sofascore) — too corporate, too light, too safe
- FIFA official app — too polished, too neutral
- Generic dark SaaS dashboards — no personality
- Neon-on-black gaming UIs — wrong energy, too synthetic

## Strategic Principles

1. The game comes first. Every screen should feel like you're IN the Copa, not managing a spreadsheet.
2. Points and rank must feel earned. The rank system (Torcedor → Lenda) is the core progression loop.
3. Mobile-first. Most users are on phones, in movement.
4. Speed. Fans won't wait. Every page should load with purpose.
